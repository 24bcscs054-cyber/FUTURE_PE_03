# Prompt 3: The SEO Content Writer (B2B Cyber Security)

**Purpose:** Use this prompt to turn your approved outlines into high-quality, authoritative blog posts that build trust with IT directors and C-level executives.

## The Prompt

> **Act as a Senior Cyber Security Copywriter specializing in B2B SEO.** > 
> Please write a comprehensive, highly engaging, and actionable blog post based exactly on the following outline: 
> 
> **[Paste the Outline Generated from Prompt 2 Here]**
> 
> **Writing & SEO Guidelines:**
> - **Readability:** Keep paragraphs short (2-4 sentences max). Use clear, professional language. Explain technical jargon (like ransomware, spear-phishing, or social engineering) in a way that non-technical business leaders can understand.
> - **Tone:** The tone must be authoritative, objective, and urgent, but **strictly avoid fear-mongering**. Focus on empowerment, risk mitigation, and building a "human firewall."
> - **Local/Target Context:** Naturally weave in local or industry-specific keywords (e.g., mentioning the business landscape in **"[Insert City]"** or the specific threats facing **"[Insert Target Industry, e.g., healthcare providers]"**) without keyword stuffing.
> - **Formatting:** Format the output cleanly using Markdown. Use bold text for key statistics, important threat vectors, and bullet points to break up dense compliance information.
> - **E-E-A-T (Critical for YMYL):** Cyber security falls under "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL). Ensure the content demonstrates high Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Cite that the information aligns with industry standards (like NIST or CIS frameworks).
